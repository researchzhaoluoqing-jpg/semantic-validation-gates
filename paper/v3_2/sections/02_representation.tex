\section{Output Representation}
\label{sec:representation}

Fix a deterministic encoder $E:\Sigma^*\to\Rd$ applied at unit level: an
output $y$ is segmented by a fixed procedure $D$ into units (sentences or
atomic claims) $u_1,\dots,u_m$.

\begin{definition}[Output measure]
$\hat\mu_y=\frac1m\sum_{j=1}^m \delta_{E(u_j)}\in\PM$.
\end{definition}

\begin{assumption}[A1]
$\|E(u)\|\le R$ for all units (normalized embeddings: $R=1$).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[A2]
All kernels are bounded ($\sup_x k(x,x)\le K$), measurable, and characteristic
on the relevant domain.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[A3]
Calibration and test outputs are exchangeable as raw texts. Used only where
stated; \S\ref{sec:regime3} removes it.
\end{assumption}

A \emph{gate} is a pair $(f_i,\tau_i)$ with measurable
$f_i:\Sigma^*\to[0,\infty)$ and threshold $\tau_i>0$; write
$\rho_i(y)=f_i(y)/\tau_i$, and gate $i$ passes iff $\rho_i(y)\le 1$. The
\emph{instrument} $I=(D,\mathrm{NLI},E,\text{graph builder})$ is fixed before
calibration and treated as part of each $f_i$; this matters for
Prop.~\ref{prop:composite}. No manifold, bundle, or Fisher-metric structure is
assumed; everything downstream lives on $(\Rd,\|\cdot\|)$ and on finite
graphs.
